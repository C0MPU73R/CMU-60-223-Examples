Assignment: One In - One Out with simple circuits

Use electricity to create a connection between two physical processes, e.g., allow one process to control or affect another. This is the essence of the single-input single-output system. Solving the task is trivial: a battery, switch, and lamp are sufficient. The hard part is identifying and achieving an interesting purpose.

One In - One Out is many things:

It is about creating a process: energy and information enters, energy and information is emitted. The circuit becomes part of a larger system.

It is about creating a relationship: otherwise disjoint elements can be connected indirectly via electricity to accomplish a human purpose.

It is about controlling time: all circuits involve delay (even if very short).

It is about crossing space: electricity is an energy form which is particularly easy to transmit from place to place.
