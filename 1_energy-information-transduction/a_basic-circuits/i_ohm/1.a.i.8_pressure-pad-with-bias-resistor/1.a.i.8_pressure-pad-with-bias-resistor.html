Exercise: Pressure pad with bias resistor

Objective: convert mechanical pressure into a varying voltage.

A pressure pad (i.e. force-sensing resistor, or FSR) is a contact sensor in which the resistance changes in response to mechanical pressure. The resistance decreases with increased force.

A voltage divider circuit can be used for forming a voltage which depends on the pressure.

Steps and observations.

  1. Measure the resistance of the pressure pad over different pressure conditions.
  2. Measure the pressure pad voltage in the given circuit over different pressure conditions.
  3. Using the oscilloscope to observe the pressure pad voltage, try creating rapidly changing contact to estimate the response speed.

Comments

FSRs can be damaged by excessive force. In practice, it can take care to design them within a mechanical assembly so they are not directly exposed to excessive force or forces which pull them apart. But they are a relatively cheap and robust way to make an analog human interface.
