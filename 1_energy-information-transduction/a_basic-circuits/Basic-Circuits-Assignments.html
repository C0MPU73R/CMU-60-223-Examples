Project Assignment: One In - One Out with simple circuits

The project assignments are intended to be performed by groups of students.

Use electricity to create a connection between two physical processes, e.g., allow one process to control or affect another. This is the essence of the single-input single-output system. Solving the task is trivial: a battery, switch, and lamp are sufficient. The hard part is identifying and achieving an interesting purpose.

One In - One Out is many things:

  1. It is about creating a process: energy and information enters, energy and information is emitted. The circuit becomes part of a larger system.
  2. It is about creating a relationship: otherwise disjoint elements can be connected indirectly via electricity to accomplish a human purpose.
  3. It is about controlling time: all circuits involve delay (even if very short).
  4. It is about crossing space: electricity is an energy form which is particularly easy to transmit from place to place.

Challenges

Any one who completes the basic exercises should consider undertaking some of the optional challenge exercises.

  1. Heat-sink a resistor to keep it intact for higher voltages in the smoke-a-resistor exercise.
  2. Create a sensor, i.e. a transducer converting a signal from one form of energy to another. Hint: the input or output could involve mechanical, electrical, thermal, or optical energy. Mechanical energy includes pneumatics and sound.
  3. Create an actuator, i.e., a transducer converting energy from one form to another to create a change in the world. Hint: consider all possible forms of energy for both representing the control signal and performing the action. Hint: if you wrap wire around a rod of metal and pass current through it you get a magnet.
  4. Create a system with time-varying behavior. Hint: the dynamics can stem from either the electrical or mechanical domain. E.g., clocks, pendula, hourglasses, discharging capacitors, low-frequency oscillators, etc.
