Objective

Use an op-amp circuit to match a sensor signal output to a driver signal input.

Many physical computing projects face the problem of translating an analog voltage from one range to another. For example, the Sharp range finders put out a signal from 0 to 2.5V, but the MOSFETs turn-on voltage is closer to 3.5-4.0V. An effective way to match one signal type to another is to add an amplifier stage which applies a gain and offset using a linear op-amp, short for “operational amplifier.” We have the TL072P op-amp in stock, which includes two op-amps in an 8-pin package.

Many op-amp circuits are designed for a dual supply, meaning they assume the availability of matched positive and negative supplies centered around the zero ground voltage. However, it is more convenient for our purposes to just use a single supply, so some care may be required when using cookbook circuits.

Steps and observations

  1. Wire up the circuit, trying to use only the schematic.
  2. Modulate the distance of the object nearest the sensor, observing the behavior of the non-inverting and inverting output.
